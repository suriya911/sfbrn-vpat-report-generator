## Summary

**Classification: Need TAAP**

This Cornerstone Learn VPAT documents a product with **significant accessibility barriers** that require a formal remediation and accessibility action plan before deployment or continued use.

### Key Findings:

1. **Low Compliance Score**: 44% of reviewable criteria are supported (8 of 18), indicating widespread accessibility gaps.

2. **Multiple Level A and AA Failures**: The VPAT marks 14 Level A criteria and 10 Level AA criteria as "Partially Supports," with documented exceptions affecting:
   - Keyboard access and focus management
   - Screen reader compatibility
   - Form labels and error messages
   - Color contrast
   - Reflow and text spacing
   - Status message announcements

3. **Core Workflow Barriers**: Users relying on keyboard-only navigation, screen readers, or magnification cannot access critical learning management functions (navigation, forms, status updates, dragging operations).

4. **Incomplete Testing**: Evaluation limited to Chrome + JAWS; no testing with other browsers or assistive technologies.

5. **No Remediation Plan**: VPAT provides no timeline or roadmap for fixing documented issues.

### Regulatory Impact:
- **ADA**: Barriers prevent equal access for employees and learners with disabilities.
- **Section 508**: Failures in Functional Performance Criteria for vision, color perception, and manipulation.
- **WCAG 2.0/2.1/2.2 AA**: Multiple criteria not met.

**Recommendation**: Require vendor to provide a detailed TAAP with prioritized remediation timelines. Do not deploy until Level A and AA barriers are resolved and verified through expanded testing.
